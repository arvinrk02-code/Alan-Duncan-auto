# PRODUCT.md — Alan Duncan Garage Services

**Register:** brand (marketing site; the design *is* the product).
**Type:** one-page local-business site for an independent garage.

## Who / where / job
Alan Duncan runs an independent garage at 7 Industrial Estate, Dufftown, Moray
(AB55 4FR) — Speyside, rural north-east Scotland. Servicing, repairs, MOT work
for all makes, plus a few used cars for sale. Trade is local and word-of-mouth.

**The page's single job:** get a local driver to phone the garage (or drop in)
with confidence — and rank for "garage / car repair / MOT Dufftown & Moray".

## Audience
Local drivers, many not young, on phones. They want: is this place trustworthy,
what do they do, are they open, where are they, what's the number. No fluff.

## Voice (three physical words)
**Honest · hard-wearing · hands-on.** Reference objects: a well-thumbed Haynes
workshop manual, a car dashboard lit at night, an enamel garage forecourt sign.
Copy is plain and short. No buzzwords, no "leverage/seamless/solutions". Say what
they do and what it costs you to call. Warmth comes from tone + colour, not adjectives.

## Non-negotiables
- Real brand: their orange + green + charcoal + amber (from sign & business card).
- Their motifs: dashboard warning lights, spark plug, "For all your car needs".
- SEO: local keywords, AutoRepair + FAQ schema, fast, semantic, accessible.
- Honesty: no fabricated reviews. Real sentiment only, clearly sourced.
- Mobile-first, AA contrast, reduced-motion safe.

## Success = the AI-slop test passes
A visitor should think "a real person made this for a real garage", not
"which AI made this". Distinctive, specific, restrained where it counts.
